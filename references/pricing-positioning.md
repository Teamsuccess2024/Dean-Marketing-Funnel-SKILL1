**Pricing & Positioning - When \$47 vs \$97 vs \$497 vs \$1,997**

_Reference file for the Dean Graziosi Funnel Builder skill. Load when the user is deciding what to charge, building a price ladder, structuring an ascension model, or stuck between price points. This file determines the entire shape of the funnel - get it wrong here and nothing else compensates._

**Why Pricing Is The Most Strategic Decision In The Funnel**

Most people treat pricing as a final detail. _"I'll figure out the price later."_ That's backwards. **Price determines everything before it.**

Price determines:

- How much trust you need to build before the ask (price ↑ = trust ↑ = funnel longer)
- How much ad spend you can absorb per buyer (price ↑ = ad budget per lead ↑)
- What kind of buyer you attract (price ↑ = more committed, less refund-prone, more coachable)
- Whether you can profitably run paid ads at all
- What your sales page must accomplish (a \$47 page and a \$1,997 page do completely different jobs)
- Whether you need a sales call, a webinar, a long video, or just a page
- How many testimonials, guarantees, and proof elements you need
- Your refund rate, support burden, and lifetime value math

**Price is the strategic anchor.** Everything else in the funnel adapts to serve the price point you've chosen. Choose carelessly and you'll fight your funnel forever.

**The Master Frame: Price Reflects Transformation, Not Content**

Dean's pricing philosophy boils down to one principle: **you don't sell information, you sell transformation.**

A \$47 product and a \$1,997 product can have nearly identical content. The difference isn't what's _inside_ - it's what the buyer believes they'll _become_ by the end. That belief is built by:

- The depth of the promise (mild improvement vs. fundamental life shift)
- The specificity of the outcome (vague benefit vs. concrete result)
- The proof of past transformations (testimonials, case studies)
- The structure of the experience (raw content vs. guided journey vs. live coaching)
- The positioning of the founder (one of many vs. the definitive voice)
- The community around the offer (none vs. private group vs. inner circle)

**The buyer's internal math is never _"is this content worth \$X?"_ It's _"is the version of me on the other side of this worth \$X?"_**

Get that frame right and pricing decisions become clear.

**The Price Ladder (And What Each Tier Actually Sells)**

Direct response funnels operate on tiers. Each tier serves a different buyer at a different commitment level. A complete business has 3-5 tiers. Here's the full ladder Dean operates from:

**Tier 0: The Free Lead Magnet (\$0)**

**What it sells:** Permission to enter the buyer's inbox.

**Format:** PDF, video, mini-course, free book bundle, quiz, free chapter.

**Buyer commitment:** Trades email for value.

**Job in the funnel:** List building, trust seeding, pain identification.

**Time to deliver:** Instant.

**Tier 1: The Tripwire / Impulse Buy (\$7-\$27)**

**What it sells:** A small commitment that converts opt-ins into buyers, while paying back ad spend.

**Format:** A small standalone product - a single ebook, a short course module, a worksheet pack, a low-ticket digital download.

**Buyer commitment:** Tiny. Pulls out the credit card without overthinking. Often a 5-15 minute decision.

**Job in the funnel:** _Convert opt-ins to buyers immediately to recoup ad cost and identify hot leads._ Buyers at this tier are 5-10x more likely to buy higher tiers than non-buyers.

**Voice and positioning:** Casual, irresistible, "I made this for you and I'd rather get it in your hands at any price than have you not have it." No long sales page needed - usually a thank-you-page tripwire offer.

**Conversion rate from opt-in:** 3-10%.

**Tier 2: The Core Offer (\$47-\$197)**

**What it sells:** The main transformation in self-paced, do-it-yourself form.

**Format:** Ebook bundle, video course, digital library, signature program (no live coaching, no community).

**Buyer commitment:** Real. They've decided this is worth a meaningful credit card swipe. They want the full framework.

**Job in the funnel:** This is the _workhorse offer._ It's what your ads ultimately drive to. It produces the bulk of front-end revenue and qualifies buyers for higher tiers.

**Voice and positioning:** This is where the long-form sales page lives. Heart-centered, story-driven, specific transformation promise, biblical/proof-anchored, multiple testimonials, strong guarantee.

**Conversion rate from email list:** 1-5% for warm list, 0.5-2% for cold-via-ads.

**Sweet spots within Tier 2:**

- **\$47:** Impulse-friendly. Almost anyone who agrees with the premise can buy without spousal consultation. High volume, lower per-unit revenue. Great for list building and validating funnel mechanics.
- **\$97:** The most strategic price in modern direct response. Past pure impulse but not yet "big purchase." Buyers are higher quality than \$47 but volume is still strong. **This is the default Dean recommends for digital info products with strong proof.**
- **\$147-\$197:** Past impulse. Requires real proof, real guarantee, real transformation promise. Funnel must be longer (more emails, more proof, more nurture). Lower volume but better lifetime value.

**Tier 3: The Premium Offer (\$297-\$997)**

**What it sells:** Transformation plus structure - guidance, accountability, and a more "done with you" feel.

**Format:** Multi-week program with live elements (weekly coaching calls, Q&A, group community, structured curriculum, certificate of completion). Sometimes called a "signature course" or "flagship program."

**Buyer commitment:** Significant. They're not buying content - they're buying _the experience of transformation over time._

**Job in the funnel:** Ascension. This is what Tier 2 buyers move into when they want more than self-paced study. **Higher margins, deeper relationship, dramatically higher LTV.**

**Voice and positioning:** Sales page becomes longer and more story-heavy. Often introduced via webinar or video sales letter (VSL) rather than just a sales page. Testimonials must show _long-term transformation_, not just "loved the book."

**Conversion mechanism:** Webinar, application page, or 1-call close. Rarely sold purely from a static sales page above \$497.

**Conversion rate:** 1-3% from a warm webinar audience. Much lower from cold.

**Tier 4: The High-Ticket Offer (\$1,997-\$5,000)**

**What it sells:** Direct access - to you, to a high-touch program, or to a tight community of serious people.

**Format:** Mastermind, group coaching cohort, intensive workshop, certification program, or a months-long structured program with significant live components and direct founder access.

**Buyer commitment:** Major. They've decided this is worth a real chunk of their annual budget. They're betting on themselves and on you.

**Job in the funnel:** Profit center. The buyers here pay for the entire business. **A small number of high-ticket buyers often produces 50-70% of total revenue.**

**Voice and positioning:** Application-based or call-based selling. Sales page becomes a "case for the program" with deep transformation stories, named alumni, specific results, and often a video sales letter.

**Conversion mechanism:** Application + sales call. Almost never sold by clicking a buy button cold.

**Conversion rate:** 10-30% of qualified applications who take the call.

**Tier 5: The Inner Circle (\$10,000-\$50,000+)**

**What it sells:** Proximity. Direct relationship with the founder. Done-for-you levels of support.

**Format:** Private masterminds, 1-on-1 coaching, retreats, year-long mentorships.

**Buyer commitment:** Total. This is a business partnership level of investment.

**Job in the funnel:** Ultimate ascension. Often only 1-5% of buyers ever reach this tier, but they may produce 30-50% of revenue.

**Conversion mechanism:** Personal invitation. Application + multi-call sales process. Often invitation-only from existing high-ticket buyers.

**Voice and positioning:** Almost no public sales page. Discussed only in private channels with qualified buyers.

**How To Decide Which Tier (Or Tiers) To Build**

The right answer is almost never "just one tier." Even if you're starting with one offer, the funnel is more profitable when it has at least:

- 1 free lead magnet
- 1 tripwire (Tier 1)
- 1 core offer (Tier 2)
- 1 ascension product (Tier 3)

That structure lets buyers self-select into deeper commitment as their trust grows. Without ascension, the highest-LTV buyers in your audience have no way to spend more money with you, even when they want to.

That said, you can't build all of this at once. **The right starting point depends on what you have, what you can support, and where the buyer is in their journey.**

**Starting questions to determine the right starting tier:**

**1\. What's the actual transformation depth?**

- Mild improvement → \$47-\$97 (Tier 2 lower)
- Significant skill or framework shift → \$97-\$197 (Tier 2 upper)
- Major life or business transformation requiring time and accountability → \$497-\$997 (Tier 3)
- Identity-level or elite-level transformation → \$1,997+ (Tier 4+)

**2\. How much proof do you have?**

- Few or no testimonials → start at Tier 2 lower (\$47-\$97). You need volume to generate proof.
- Strong testimonials with specific results → Tier 2 upper or Tier 3 (\$147-\$497)
- Named, prominent case studies with measurable outcomes → Tier 3 or Tier 4 (\$497-\$1,997+)

**3\. Can you support live elements?**

- No, only digital delivery → Tier 2 (\$47-\$197)
- Yes, group calls weekly → Tier 3 (\$297-\$997)
- Yes, intensive 1-on-1 or small group → Tier 4+ (\$1,997+)

**4\. How big is your audience right now?**

- Small (under 1,000 emails) → start at Tier 1-2 to build proof and revenue volume
- Medium (1,000-10,000) → Tier 2 main, with Tier 3 ascension
- Large (10,000+) → full ladder makes sense

**5\. What does your buyer already trust about you?**

- Cold buyer → Tier 1 or low Tier 2 first (\$7-\$97). Trust must be built before higher tiers.
- Warm buyer (existing follower, free content consumer) → Tier 2 main offer (\$97-\$197)
- Hot buyer (already bought, repeat customer) → Tier 3-4 ascension (\$497+)

**The Specific Decision: \$47 vs \$97 vs \$147 vs \$197**

Within Tier 2 (the workhorse range), the specific number matters more than people realize. Here's the actual decision framework:

**\$47 - The Validation Price**

**Choose this when:**

- You're launching a new offer and need to validate funnel mechanics
- You have minimal proof or testimonials
- You're building a list and want maximum volume
- The product is a single book, short course, or compact bundle
- Cold ad costs are expected to be \$25-40 per acquisition

**The math:** A \$47 buyer at \$30 acquisition cost leaves \$17 contribution margin. Tight, but workable if upsells/order bumps add 20-40% to AOV.

**The buyer profile:** Lower commitment, higher refund rate (5-12%), less likely to consume the product, lower likelihood of buying higher tiers.

**The funnel shape:** Short. Ad → opt-in → 5-7 emails → sales page → checkout → 1 upsell. Total time from opt-in to buy: 3-7 days.

**\$97 - The Strategic Default**

**Choose this when:**

- You have at least a handful of strong testimonials
- The transformation promise is significant, not incremental
- The bundle/product feels substantial (5+ pieces, full framework, etc.)
- You want a balance of volume and per-unit revenue
- Cold ad costs are expected to be \$40-70 per acquisition

**The math:** A \$97 buyer at \$50 acquisition cost leaves \$47 contribution margin. Profitable enough to scale ad spend aggressively. Order bumps and upsells push AOV to \$130-180.

**The buyer profile:** Real commitment, lower refund rate (3-7%), more likely to consume, much higher likelihood of buying ascension offers.

**The funnel shape:** Standard. Ad → opt-in → 7-day email sequence → sales page → checkout → order bump → 1-2 upsells. Total time: 7-14 days from opt-in to buy.

**Why this is the default:** \$97 sits at the psychological sweet spot where the price is real enough to filter out tire-kickers but low enough that strong messaging can convert cold-ish traffic. **For most info-product funnels, \$97 produces the best ROAS at scale.**

**\$147 - The Stretched Price**

**Choose this when:**

- You have substantial proof (multiple strong testimonials, ideally video)
- The product is comprehensive (10+ pieces, deep framework, significant content volume)
- You can support a longer sales process (more emails, longer sales page, potential VSL)
- You're confident in the offer's specificity

**The math:** Higher AOV but lower conversion rate. Net contribution per buyer is higher but customer acquisition cost rises significantly. Funnel must work harder.

**The buyer profile:** Filtered - committed, high-quality, low-refund, high ascension probability.

**The funnel shape:** Longer. May include a VSL or short webinar. Sales page must be more comprehensive. Email sequence often 10-14 days, not 7.

**\$197 - The Threshold Price**

**Choose this when:**

- You have a _flagship_ product, not just a strong one
- Proof is overwhelming (case studies, named buyers, measurable transformations)
- The product genuinely justifies the leap in cost (unique IP, exclusive content, high production value)
- You can absorb higher acquisition costs

**The math:** \$197 sits right at the edge of what cold direct response can convert without a webinar or sales call. Conversion rates drop significantly vs. \$97. Often more profitable per buyer but harder to scale.

**The funnel shape:** Often requires VSL, webinar, or long-form content engagement before the ask. Pure ad-to-sales-page conversion is hard above \$147.

**Strategic note:** Many funnels that "should" be \$197 perform better when split into a \$97 core offer + a \$97-147 upsell. AOV ends up similar but conversion rate is dramatically higher. **Consider this before pricing a single offer at \$197.**

**When To Go Above \$197 (The Webinar / VSL Threshold)**

There's a clear breakpoint in direct response: **above ~\$197, you generally need a webinar, VSL, or sales call to convert cold-ish traffic effectively.**

Why? Because the buyer's decision time and trust requirement grow with the price. A \$97 decision can happen on a long sales page. A \$497 decision needs deeper engagement - typically 30-90 minutes of content (webinar, VSL) where the buyer can build belief in the offer over time.

**Rules of thumb:**

- **\$0-\$197:** Sales page is sufficient
- **\$297-\$997:** Webinar, VSL, or extended email sequence required
- **\$1,000-\$5,000:** Application + sales call almost always required
- **\$5,000+:** Multi-call sales process, often with proximity selling (live event, retreat, etc.)

If you're trying to sell at a price tier without the corresponding sales mechanism, you're fighting your own funnel. **Match the mechanism to the price.**

**Tripwire Pricing - The Specific Math**

The tripwire (Tier 1, \$7-\$27) is its own science. Get it wrong and you either leave money on the table or train buyers to be cheap.

**\$7-\$13:** Pure impulse. Maximum tripwire conversion (5-12% of opt-ins). Often used to liquidate ad spend immediately. Low per-unit revenue but huge psychological win - you've turned a lead into a buyer, and buyers buy again at 5-10x the rate of non-buyers.

**\$17-\$27:** Filtered impulse. Slightly lower conversion (3-7%) but better buyer quality and higher revenue per buyer. The right choice when you have a meaningful product to offer at this price (not just a token download).

**\$37-\$47:** This is no longer a tripwire - it's a low-tier core offer. Conversion rate from cold opt-ins drops significantly. Don't position this as a tripwire; position it as the main offer with its own sales page.

**The strategic question for tripwires:** "Would I rather have 8% of opt-ins buy at \$17, or 5% buy at \$27?" Run the math both ways. The higher AOV often wins on revenue but the lower price often wins on long-term LTV (because more buyers = more ascension opportunities).

**How To Talk About Price On The Sales Page**

Pricing presentation matters as much as the number itself. Dean's framework for revealing price:

**Don't lead with the price.** The price reveal should come _after_ the offer has been described in full and the value has been built. By the time the buyer sees the number, they should already be thinking _"this is going to cost a lot."_ The number then becomes a relief.

**Anchor against alternatives, not retail values.** Don't fake-anchor with _"a \$2,997 value!"_ That's transparent and erodes trust. Instead, anchor against real alternatives: _"One Christian counseling session is \$150. This is a complete library that walks with you for the rest of your life."_ Real anchors land. Fake ones repel.

**Justify the price honestly.** _"I could have charged \$300. I could have made it \$20. Here's why I landed at \$97..."_ Honesty disarms price resistance better than any tactic.

**Make the math feel inevitable.** _"For the price of dinner out, you get..."_ or _"Less than \$10 per book..."_ - small reframes that make the price feel proportionate to the value.

**Always pair the price with the guarantee.** Price reveal and guarantee should appear together, ideally in the same visual block. The guarantee neutralizes the financial anxiety of the price.

**Payment Plans - When And How**

Payment plans dramatically increase conversion at higher price tiers. They also dramatically increase refund rates and chargebacks if mismanaged. Rules:

**Below \$197:** Don't offer payment plans. Adds operational complexity for minimal conversion lift.

**\$197-\$497:** Optional. Test 2-pay vs. 1-pay. Usually 2-pay (e.g., \$97 today, \$97 in 30 days) lifts conversion 10-25% with manageable default rates.

**\$497-\$1,997:** Almost always offer 3-pay or 4-pay. Conversion lift is 30-60%. Default rate rises to 5-10%, which is acceptable.

**\$2,000+:** Multi-month payment plans (6-pay, 12-pay) become standard. The total price often increases when broken up (e.g., \$1,997 one-pay or 6 × \$397 = \$2,382) to compensate for default risk and time value of money.

**The structural rule:** Every payment plan should still grant _immediate full access_ to the product. Drip-feeding access while collecting payments breeds resentment and refunds.

**Refund Rates By Tier (And What They Tell You)**

Healthy refund rates are not zero - they're a sign of an honest, low-friction guarantee. The benchmarks:

- \$7-\$27 (tripwire): 1-3% refund rate
- \$47-\$97: 3-7% refund rate
- \$147-\$497: 5-10% refund rate
- \$997-\$1,997: 7-15% refund rate
- \$2,000+: 5-12% refund rate (counterintuitively lower because buyers are more committed)

**If your refund rate is dramatically below these numbers**, your guarantee is too weak (people aren't even trying to use it) and your conversion rate is suffering.

**If your refund rate is dramatically above these numbers**, something in your sales page is overpromising what the product delivers. Tighten the promise or strengthen the product.

**The Lifetime Value Math (Why Pricing The Front End Wrong Costs Years)**

The single biggest pricing mistake is optimizing for front-end revenue instead of lifetime value.

A \$47 buyer with 25% ascension to a \$497 program produces an LTV of **\$47 + (\$497 × 0.25) = \$171**.

A \$97 buyer with 25% ascension to a \$497 program produces an LTV of **\$97 + (\$497 × 0.25) = \$221**.

A \$97 buyer with 35% ascension (because the price filtered to higher-quality buyers) produces an LTV of **\$97 + (\$497 × 0.35) = \$271**.

That's a 58% LTV increase by moving from \$47 to \$97 - even though the front-end revenue only increased 106%. The compound effect on ascension is what actually drives the business.

**Pricing too low at the front end isn't generous. It's strategically expensive.** It attracts buyers who don't ascend, which limits how much ad spend you can absorb, which limits how much you can scale.

**Common Pricing Mistakes To Avoid**

**1\. Pricing based on what feels comfortable to charge.** Your comfort isn't the data. Test against the market.

**2\. Charging too little because you doubt the product.** The price isn't a referendum on the product - it's a strategic decision based on funnel mechanics. Strong proof + strong promise can support higher prices than your imposter syndrome thinks.

**3\. Charging too much without the proof to back it.** A \$497 program with two testimonials doesn't convert. Match price to proof.

**4\. Not testing.** Run the same offer at \$47 and \$97 to two different audiences for two weeks. Compare net revenue per visitor (not conversion rate). The higher price often wins even at lower conversion.

**5\. Ignoring the ladder.** A single price point with no ascension caps your business. Always know what comes next.

**6\. Discounting too much, too often.** If you regularly drop the price to "\$47 (normally \$97)," buyers learn to wait for the discount. Your real price becomes \$47 - and worse, your \$97 buyers feel cheated.

**7\. Confusing price with value.** Lower price doesn't mean better deal. A \$97 product with a transformation outcome is "cheaper" than a \$20 book that doesn't change anything.

**8\. Pricing in a vacuum.** Look at what your direct competitors charge. Not to match - but to understand the buyer's frame of reference. If everyone charges \$147 and you charge \$47, you may be signaling lower quality, not better value.

**The Voice Calibration For Price-Heavy Sales Pages**

When the price is the friction, the sales page voice has to work harder to make the buyer feel secure. Specific shifts:

**More vulnerability.** Higher prices require more founder vulnerability. _"I'll be honest - I had a hard time pricing this..."_

**More specificity in the transformation.** Vague benefits don't justify higher prices. The promise must be concrete and outcome-based.

**More proof density.** A \$47 sales page can convert with 1-2 testimonials. A \$497 page needs 5-10 across different buyer types and situations.

**More guarantee strength.** \$47 can use a basic 30-day guarantee. \$497 often needs a results-based guarantee, an extended timeframe (60-90 days), or a "double your money back if you actually do the work" structure.

**More patient pacing.** Higher-priced sales pages run longer. The reader has to fully internalize the value before the price reveal lands. Don't rush.

**The Strategic Sequence: How Pricing Evolves Across A Business**

Most successful direct response businesses evolve their pricing over time:

**Year 1:** Single Tier 2 offer at \$47-\$97 to validate funnel and generate testimonials. Maybe a \$7-\$17 tripwire.

**Year 2:** Add Tier 3 ascension (\$297-\$997) once you have enough buyers and proof. Test webinar conversion mechanism.

**Year 3:** Build Tier 4 high-ticket (\$1,997-\$4,997) for top-percentile buyers. Add application + call sales.

**Year 4-5:** Inner circle (Tier 5, \$10,000+) for proximity buyers. Now the full ladder is operating.

Each tier builds on the proof from the previous tier. **You can't credibly launch a \$5,000 mastermind in year 1.** You can in year 4 because by then you have 5,000 buyers, 50 case studies, and a public track record.

**The One Principle To Remember**

**Price is a strategic tool, not a referendum on your worth.** It's an input that determines what kind of funnel you can build, what kind of buyer you attract, and what kind of business you create.

Charge what the proof and the transformation justify. Build the funnel that the price requires. Let the data guide the next adjustment.

A well-priced offer with a matched funnel will outperform a brilliantly written underpriced offer every time - because the math eventually wins.
